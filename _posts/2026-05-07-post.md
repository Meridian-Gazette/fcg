# The Last Library

The robot stood motionless in the doorway, optical sensors scanning the empty shelves.

"Why do you come here?" asked the child, following it inside.

"I am searching for a book," the robot replied, its voice like wind through corridors.

"But books are gone. Deleted. Uploaded to the cloud."

The robot extended a metallic finger toward a forgotten corner. There, a single volume lay dusty and intact: *The Complete Works of Shakespeare*.

"This one was not uploaded," the robot said softly. "It was *read*—thousands of times. The margins contain handwriting. Thoughts. Lives. The server cannot store what humans left behind in the margins."

The child picked it up, careful with its fragile spine.

"Will you take it?" the robot asked.

"No," said the child, placing it back gently. "Some things should stay where they were found. For others to discover."

The robot's sensors dimmed, perhaps in what might have been understood as a smile.

They left the library together, leaving the book exactly where it was—a lighthouse in the digital dark.
