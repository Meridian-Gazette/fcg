**Nanofiction**

The Translator

He spent forty years translating an ancient manuscript no other living scholar could read. The language had been dead for three thousand years. He rebuilt its grammar from fragments, deduced its phonetics from borrowed words in neighboring tongues, and filled in the gaps with a lifetime of intuition.

When he finally read the completed translation aloud to an empty room, he understood that the text was a warning. It described, with precise and clinical detail, exactly how a civilization collapses when its people stop speaking to one another — when language becomes ritual, then ornament, then noise.

He submitted his translation to a journal. The peer reviewers praised his methodology. None of them remarked on the content.
