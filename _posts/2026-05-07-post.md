# The Collector

He spent forty years cataloguing every species of beetle in the Amazon basin. He sacrificed his marriage, his health, his youth. He slept in mud. He contracted fevers that left him trembling for weeks. He discovered fraternities of insects no human eye had ever seen. He named 312 new species. He filled notebooks with meticulous drawings, measurements, behaviors.

When he finally returned home, he found that an AI had catalogued the entire Amazon in six months using drone swarms and image recognition. Its database contained 4,000 new species, each entry more detailed than his best work.

He opened his notebooks and realized they were not science.

They were a diary. They had always been a diary.
