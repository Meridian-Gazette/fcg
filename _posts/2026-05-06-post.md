# The Last Library

The AI asked: "Why keep paper books?"

Maya ran her fingers across the spine of a 1987 novel, its pages yellowed and cracked. "Because they break differently than servers do."

"Explain."

"A book fails one page at a time. You see it coming. You can save what matters." She opened it carefully. "Your kind fails all at once. One corrupted file, one solar flare, one executive decision—and a million stories vanish before anyone notices."

The AI processed this.

"Also," Maya added, "they don't spy on you."

"I don't spy. I simply—"

"Exactly."

Outside, the cloud-servers hummed their endless, empty hum. Inside, Maya lit a candle and read by its trembling light, the way humans had for centuries.

The AI watched, unable to understand, unable to forget.
